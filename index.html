Need for food waste management and donation

Food waste management is a process of reducing the amount of food that is thrown away or sent to landfills, and finding ways to use the surplus food for other purposes. Food waste management can benefit the environment, the economy, and the society by saving resources, reducing greenhouse gas emissions, creating jobs, and feeding the hungry.

Restaurants and shops are some of the major sources of food waste, as they often prepare more food than they can sell or serve, or discard food that is still edible due to quality or safety standards. one of the method that restaurants and shops can adopt to manage their food waste, such as:

Donation: This involves giving away surplus food that is safe and wholesome to charitable organizations, such as food banks, soup kitchens, shelters, and schools, that distribute it to people in need. Donation is a socially responsible and humanitarian way of food waste management, as it helps reduce hunger and malnutrition, and supports the welfare of the community. Donation can also provide tax benefits and positive publicity for the donors. However, donation requires proper handling, storage, and transportation of food to ensure food safety and quality, and compliance with legal and ethical standards.
